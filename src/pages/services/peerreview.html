Peer Review & Security Consulting

Security review is a critical part of the software development process. Malicious hackers are attacking anything and everything these days. Insecure software leaves it's users vulnerable to these attacks. Software insecurity can come from something as small as forgetting to sanitize user input, to implementing a completely insecure crypto-system. These insecurities will be exploited. There is no way to prevent them altogether, the best you can do is to be proactive about security - have your product, idea, or protocol reviewed, and have your development team trained to combat security flaws.

Backed with my extensive knowledge of security and applied cryptography, I offer inexpensive security consulting. I'll review your product and provide extensive reports on the security status of the product. If I find it to be secure, you'll get to put "Approved Secure by OSSBox" graphics on your web site to increase your users' faith in your product.

Too often in the software industry, security gets overlooked in favor of functionality and cost cutting. Getting your product reviewed shows that you truly care about your users' security. I charge as little as possible for this service because I just want to help make secure software, and I believe that software developers should be rewarded for going the extra mile to make their products secure.

If you really care about your users' security, this is for you.

What You Get

  • Thorough security analysis of your software, protocol, or idea.
  • Report including a summary of attack vectors and how the threats have been mitigated.
  • Web page on ossbox.com describing the security features of your product along side our in-depth analysis.
  • Custom OSSBox Seal of Approval if we find your software to be secure.
  • Greater level of user trust because a third party is putting their name and reputation behind your product.
  • If found to be insecure, you will get tips on how to improve your software or idea.
  • Optionally, I will work with you throughout the development process to ensure security standards are being met.
